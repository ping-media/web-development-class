About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love and architectural brilliance. Construction began in 1632 and took around 22 years to complete.

The Taj Mahal stands majestically on the banks of the Yamuna River, featuring intricate carvings, calligraphy, and symmetrical gardens. Its main dome, flanked by four minarets, reflects Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. The changing hues of the monument throughout the day, especially during sunrise and sunset, captivate visitors from around the globe. More than just a stunning structure, the Taj Mahal is a poignant reminder of India’s rich history and artistic heritage. It continues to inspire awe, attracting millions of tourists every year who come to witness its timeless beauty.
